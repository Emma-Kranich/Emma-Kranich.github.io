Muhlenberg Brain Camp

Summer 2017

I was selected for a free, all-expense-paid week-long program at Muhlenberg College where I had the opportunity to conduct research with a professor on memory consolidation and PTSD (along with a small group of fellow students). It has been shown that playing Tetris in hospitals actually reduces PTSD (Read here). Going off this research, we were interested in how working memory gets consolidated. We had participants play a virtual reality car shop game, then half of the participants played a different VR tetris-like game while the other half sat in a room silently. Both groups were given a free recall test where the group sitting silently scored significantly better. However, when given a multiple choice test, surprisingly, both groups scored the same. Although just a small scale study, the results demonstrated that memories can be hidden from retrieval without being erased by using a visuospatial distractor during the consolidation phase. At the end of the week, we had a poster presentation where I learned how to create a poster as well as present it. Following the conclusion of brain camp, the research was picked up by Muhlenberg students to test on a larger scale.
